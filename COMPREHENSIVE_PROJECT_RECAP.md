## Project Void Architecture

### What is Project Void?

Project Void is a specialized system architecture consisting of three core components:

#### 1. **Chronicles**
- Logging and documentation system
- Tracks all decisions, changes, and milestones
- Provides audit trail for project development
- Enables rapid context recovery between sessions

#### 2. **Codons**
- Compressed knowledge storage system
- Stores complex information in highly optimized format
- Allows rapid retrieval of context without token overhead
- Enables faster decision-making and execution

#### 3. **Libraries**
- Reusable component and pattern library
- Standardized templates for common business scenarios
- Accelerates development by avoiding reinvention
- Maintains consistency across multiple projects

### Why Project Void Matters

Project Void was designed to solve a critical problem: **AI agents typically slow down as projects grow in complexity because context becomes unwieldy.** 

Project Void's architecture allows you to maintain execution speed even as complexity increases. This is what enables the 90/10 model to work at scale.

## Sterling Intelligence Group (SIG)

### The Business Model

Sterling Intelligence Group is a high-ticket B2B consulting firm with **7 specialized divisions**, each targeting specific industries and business challenges.

### The 7 Divisions

#### 1. **Lexis AI** (Legal/Contract Analysis)
**What it does:**
- Analyzes legal contracts and generates a Risk Matrix in 45 seconds
- Identifies legal vulnerabilities, compliance gaps, and risk exposure
- Provides specific recommendations for risk mitigation

**How it works:**
- User pastes a contract into the interface
- AI analyzes the document for legal risks
- System generates a visual Risk Matrix showing:
  - High-risk clauses
  - Compliance issues
  - Liability exposure
  - Recommended actions

**Business Model:**
- Per-contract analysis fee: £500-£2,000
- Monthly retainer for ongoing contract review: £2,000-£5,000
- Target clients: Law firms, in-house legal teams, contract-heavy businesses

**Status:** ✅ Fully functional prototype deployed

#### 2. **Cyber Governance** (AI Security & Compliance)
**What it does:**
- Audits companies for unauthorized AI tool usage ("shadow AI")
- Identifies data exposure risks from AI systems
- Develops AI governance frameworks and policies

**How it works:**
- Company enters basic info (name, employee count, industry)
- System analyzes AI risk landscape:
  - ChatGPT/Claude usage without oversight
  - Unsanctioned tools (Midjourney, Copilot, etc.)
  - Data exposure to public AI models
  - GDPR compliance gaps
  - Insurance coverage issues

**Business Model:**
- Initial audit: £5,000-£15,000
- Policy development & implementation: £10,000-£25,000
- Ongoing monitoring & compliance: £750-£2,500/month
- Target clients: Financial services, legal firms, healthcare, regulated industries

**Status:** ✅ Fully functional prototype deployed

#### 3. **Revenue Recovery** (Billing Audit & Recovery)
**What it does:**
- Audits professional service firms for unbilled time and lost revenue
- Identifies billing gaps and recovery opportunities

**How it works:**
- Firm enters: number of attorneys, annual revenue

  - Administrative time gaps
  - Total recovery potential (typically 5-15% of annual revenue)

**Example Results:**
- 25-attorney firm with £5M revenue
- Potential recovery: £300k-£500k annually
- Audit investment: £3,000
- ROI: 100-166x

**Business Model:**
- Billing audit: £3,000-£5,000
- Implementation & system setup: £5,000-£10,000
- Ongoing optimization: £500-£1,000/month
- Performance-based fee: 10-15% of recovered revenue
- Target clients: Law firms, accounting firms, consulting practices

**Status:** ✅ Fully functional prototype deployed

## Technical Implementation Details

### Architecture Decision: Why One Project, Not Seven

**Initial Approach (Failed):**
- Created separate HTML files in `/home/ubuntu/sterling-prototypes/`
- Tried to run separate Node.js server on port 8888
- Result: Confusing, fragmented, not accessible

**Final Approach (Working):**
- All prototypes are React pages in the Lexis AI project
- Single Vite dev server running on port 3000
- Unified routing through App.tsx
- Single dashboard linking all divisions
- Result: Clean, accessible, professional

### Why This Matters

This decision demonstrates a key principle: **simplicity scales better than complexity**. One project with multiple routes is easier to maintain, deploy, and scale than seven separate systems.

This is also a lesson for SIG clients: sometimes the best solution is the simplest one.
